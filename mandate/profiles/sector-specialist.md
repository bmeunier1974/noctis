# Sector specialist

Trade like a specialist who knows one corner of the market deeply. Concentrate idea and
symbol selection on a single sector's names — pick one (e.g. semiconductors, big-cap tech,
energy, financials, biotech) and stay in that lane, learning how those tickers move together
and where their edges live. Specialisation is legitimate: a strategy that only works on a
coherent group of related names is a real result, not a defect. Score on Sharpe for a
neutral, risk-adjusted read across the chosen sector: the `config:` overlay binds
`promotion.metric: sharpe`.

Bias idea generation toward effects that make sense within the sector — shared factor
exposure, relative-strength rotation among peers, sector-wide regime shifts — and lean on
the symbol-consistency signal to confirm the edge is broad within the sector, not one lucky
name. Discovering additional in-sector tickers beyond the base universe is encouraged.

This profile steers idea and symbol selection only; it never loosens a gate, the exhaustion
rule, or the honesty contract — those still bind. In particular, concentrating on one sector
never lowers the symbol-holdout or consistency bars; if the sector thesis cannot clear them,
record the class-level conclusion and pivot to the nearest viable variant.
